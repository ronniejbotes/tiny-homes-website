# Configurator interior pairs — prompts for free generation in the Higgsfield app

Each product needs TWO images: an **empty shell** and a **furnished** version of the *same* room.

**How to run each pair (free in the app):**
1. Generate the EMPTY prompt with **Seedream 4.5** (unlimited), aspect ratio **3:2** (4:3 also fine).
2. Open **Seedream V5 Lite** (unlimited) in image-edit / reference mode, attach the empty result as the reference image, and run the FURNISHED prompt. Use the same aspect ratio if the tool allows.
3. If the furnished result morphs furniture together, re-roll it (free) — don't accept blended legs/edges.
4. Save both as JPG named `<slug>-shell-empty.jpg` and `<slug>-shell-furnished.jpg` (slugs below), drop them all in one folder (e.g. `Downloads\configurator-shells\`), and tell Claude the folder path — resizing, compression, renaming and wiring into the site is handled from there.

The X-Fold pair is already done and live — prompts included at the bottom only in case you want to redo it.

## expandable-homes — Expandable Home
*(The Expandable ships fully kitted — the "empty" state shows the included kitchen and bathroom door, just no loose furniture.)*

**EMPTY / FITTED SHELL (Seedream 4.5, 3:2):**
> Interior photograph of an expanded prefab family home, one large open-plan room about 5.8 m wide and 6.3 m deep with a 2.5 m flat ceiling, photographed from the glass sliding door looking in. Smooth white insulated wall panels with slim seams, warm oak-look SPC vinyl plank floor, white panel ceiling with two round LED lights, warm late-afternoon garden light coming in from behind the camera, one aluminium-framed double-glazed window in each side wall. Along the back wall on the LEFT: a fitted compact kitchen with light-grey cabinets, an oak-look counter, a stainless sink and a small two-plate hob. In the back RIGHT corner: a plain closed white door to the built-in bathroom. A small white electrical DB box on one wall. NO loose furniture at all — no sofa, no bed, no tables, no chairs, no rugs, no objects on the counter, no people — just the fitted kitchen, the bathroom door and open empty floor. Photoreal real-estate interior photography, realistic proportions, warm natural light. No text, no watermarks.

**FURNISHED (Seedream V5 Lite, edit with fitted shell as reference):**
> Edit this photo: keep the exact same room, camera angle, walls, windows, fitted kitchen, bathroom door, ceiling, lights and oak floor — change nothing structural and do not alter the kitchen. Add ONLY these five separate furniture items, each clearly apart with visible floor gaps, none touching another: (1) a light-grey three-seater sofa against the left wall in front of the window; (2) a low oak coffee table in front of the sofa with a clear gap; (3) a small oak dining table with four chairs in the middle-right area; (4) a neatly made double bed with cream duvet and forest-green throw in the far right area past the dining table; (5) a cream rug flat on the floor under the coffee table. Each object crisp and realistic with correct separate legs, straight edges and natural shadows. No other furniture, no people. Same warm lighting and colour grade, photoreal, seamless edit. No text, no watermarks.

**POOF VIDEO (Seedance, first frame = fitted shell, last frame = furnished, 4 s, 4:3):**
> Locked-off tripod shot, camera completely static, no zoom, no pan, no drift. The room stays exactly the same — walls, fitted kitchen, bathroom door, windows, floor, ceiling and lighting never change. One by one, five pieces of furniture magically appear in quick succession, each materialising instantly out of a small soft white puff of smoke, like a stage magician's trick: first the grey sofa on the left, then the coffee table and rug in front of it, then the dining table with its four chairs, and finally the double bed on the far right. Each poof is small, quick and playful — the smoke bursts, the object is simply there, and the smoke thins and vanishes completely within half a second. Furniture appears fully formed and solid, no warping, no growing, no melting. By the final frame every piece stands in place and all smoke is gone. Photoreal, warm interior light throughout, subtle natural shadows under each object. No people, no text, no watermarks, no camera movement.
